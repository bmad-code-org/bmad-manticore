Scenario 01  ·  Setup day

The onboarding interview

🎬
You say, on a fresh installTalk to Manny.
Manny does
  • Notices the studio is not built yet and routes you to mc-setup
  • Checks dependencies and your platform (uv, ffmpeg, node, git)
  • Interviews you: identity, editor, render consent, and a video style pass that seeds your Production Bible
  • Asks for creators you want to emulate (drop video links) and echoes back the takeaway for you to confirm
  • Builds your brand, indexes your headshots, runs a guided voice bible, registers your tools
You approve
  • Any dependency installs before they run
  • Your render-first default (preview every iteration, final offered)
  • Visual density, image-type policy, and CTA appetite
  • Which sources feed the voice bible
You get
  • A configured studio the whole pipeline reads
  • Production Bible, voice bible, brand tokens, blacklist
  • Approved, expression-indexed headshots
  • An honest runnability report of what is ready and what is pending
Scenario 02  ·  Talking head

An idea in your head, start to finish

💡
You sayManny, I've got an idea for a video.
Manny does
  • Scaffolds a talking-head project, then brain dumps with you until your phrasing repeats (camera optional)
  • Drafts hooks and an outline, then weaves the script from your own words
  • After you record, cuts word-level, plans graphics beats and CTAs, farms assets, packages
You approve
  • Gate 1 hook and outline
  • Gate 2 the cut plan, with a preview render
  • Gate 3 the beat table and CTAs, riffed with you before they are written
  • Gate 4 the offered final render
You get
  • A script that sounds like you, because it is you
  • A watchable preview at every step
  • Brand-themed overlays and CTAs anchored to your words
  • A final render plus an always-written editor timeline
Scenario 03  ·  Footage-first

You already have the footage

🎥
You sayMake a video from this recording. I already have the take.
Manny does
  • Routes to ingest mode: registers your source and skips ideation entirely
  • Starts the project at the cut stage, transcribing word-level with verbatim fillers
  • Cuts dead air and filler, marks chapters, then runs the same stages from cut onward
You approve
  • Gate 2 the cut plan, one taste call at a time
  • Gate 3 the beats and CTAs mined from the transcript
  • Gate 4 the offered final render
You get
  • A tight cut of a conference talk, raw take, or long recording
  • The same preview render and editor timeline exports
  • Graphics, packaging, and chapters, no re-shoot required
Scenario 04  ·  The weekly livestream

Two lanes, one show

Before the stream

The branded stream pack

"Build me a stream pack for tonight."

Manny does: routes to the stream-pack lane, a complete branded OBS asset pack

You approve: the scene overlays and lower thirds against your brand

You get: a ready-to-load OBS pack, click-to-trigger live graphics

After the stream

The VOD lane

"Turn last night's stream into a video."

Manny does: footage-first ingest on the livestream-vod format, transcript analysis, light trim, CTA and overlay insertion per your Production Bible, shorts mining

You approve: the same gates from the cut onward

You get: a packaged VOD with dual-timeline chapters, plus mined shorts

Scenario 05  ·  Packaging

Titles and thumbnails that land

🎭
You sayLet's nail the title and thumbnail before I forget.
Manny does
  • Packaging can run any time after gate 1, so titles never pile up at the end
  • Builds face-plus-hook thumbnails from your approved headshots
  • For a series, generates three title and thumbnail pairs for A/B testing
  • Runs the 120px test: if it does not read tiny, it does not ship
You approve
  • The winning title from the candidates
  • The thumbnail that reads at a glance
  • Description and chapters
You get
  • One blessed asset per slot, alternates kept aside in work
  • A packaging promise you set before the script was written
  • Titles, thumbnail, description, and chapters, ready to publish
Scenario 06  ·  The retro loop

The pipeline gets smarter every video

📈
You say, after publishingHere's what worked and what I'd change next time.
Manny does
  • Routes each note to the file that would have prevented it
  • Voice miss goes to the voice bible, a phrase you never say goes to the blacklist
  • Visual-style miss goes to the Production Bible, a structure miss to the format profile
  • Offers the post-publish wrap: archive hygiene, promoting evergreen assets
You approve
  • Each edit to your own taste files
  • The ratchet only turns one way: bibles and blacklists grow, gates never weaken
You get
  • Taste that lives in files and compounds with you
  • A next video that starts smarter than this one
  • A pipeline that gets cheaper to run without getting worse
